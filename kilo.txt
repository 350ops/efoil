The “dilemma” is not that you need a pivot — it’s that you need a clean separation of funnels so tourists can buy fast without your yacht page looking like a tour kiosk.

What I’d change (brand-safe, cashflow-positive)

1) Add a dedicated “Hulhumalé Sessions” money page (not “rentals”)

Right now your homepage is doing too much. Create a page that’s explicitly for local sessions:

Add route:
src/app/hulhumale/page.tsx (or /sessions/hulhumale)

SEO title example:
“eFoil Hulhumalé | Private Sessions Near Malé”

Tone: concierge/private, not “activity shop”.

This lets you run ads and IG traffic to a page that converts without touching the yacht positioning.

Also add it to routes in src/resources/once-ui.config.ts (you already centrally control route enablement there).

⸻

2) Split your homepage CTA into two doors (you already have the components)

In src/app/page.tsx, you already use HeroCTA and BottomCTA. Make the hero CTA two buttons:
	•	Yacht Delivery → /yachts
	•	Hulhumalé Sessions → /hulhumale (or /book/location if that’s your booking flow)

That’s the simplest way to preserve luxury perception while letting regular customers self-select.

⸻

3) Keep the nav premium, but include sessions as “Experiences”

Your work label is currently "Experience" in src/resources/content.tsx. Keep that, but ensure your “Experience” section includes:
	•	Yacht delivery (already)
	•	Hulhumalé private sessions
	•	Crew layover trips (/crew)
	•	“Private day experiences” (curated)

Avoid adding a top-level nav item called “Rentals”.

⸻

4) Change one phrase that currently weakens yacht credibility

On the homepage metadata you’re using:
	•	openGraph.title: "eFoil Rentals Maldives"

That word “Rentals” is fine for Google, but for yachts it can read “tourist rental shop”.

Fix: keep SEO value but make it sound premium:
	•	“eFoil Experiences Maldives” (OG)
	•	Keep “rental” in body/FAQ where needed for search intent

Where: src/app/page.tsx inside generateMetadata().

⸻

5) Don’t send yacht traffic to /work unless /work is yacht-clean

Your yacht page CTA currently sends to /work (“View Packages”). If /work contains touristy pricing/cards, captains will bounce.

Best practice:
	•	Yacht CTA → /yachts#packages (yacht-specific packages on same page)
	•	Tourist CTA → /hulhumale or /book/location

So: either move yacht packages into yachts/page.tsx, or make /work clearly segmented into “Yacht / Resort / Hulhumalé”.
