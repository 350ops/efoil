## What Is an eFoil?

An eFoil (short for electric hydrofoil) is a surfboard with a battery-powered motor and a hydrofoil wing mounted underneath. When you ride it, the wing generates lift as it moves through water, raising the board—and you—above the surface. The result is a smooth, silent glide that feels like flying.

Unlike traditional surfing, you do not need waves. Unlike jet skis, there is no engine noise. The eFoil is controlled by a wireless handheld remote that lets you adjust speed precisely, making it accessible for beginners while still thrilling for experienced riders.

## How Does an eFoil Work?

An eFoil has four main components:

- **Board** — A buoyant deck, typically made from carbon fibre or fibreglass, where you stand.
- **Mast** — A vertical strut that connects the board to the hydrofoil wing below the surface. Mast lengths vary (usually 60–90 cm), affecting ride height and stability.
- **Hydrofoil wing** — A submerged wing that generates lift as water flows over it. Larger wings offer more stability at lower speeds, while smaller wings allow sharper turns.
- **Electric motor and battery** — A brushless motor housed in the mast or wing drives a propeller. A rechargeable lithium battery inside the board powers it, typically lasting 60–90 minutes per charge.

When you squeeze the throttle on the hand controller, the motor accelerates the board. At around 15–20 km/h, the hydrofoil generates enough lift to raise the board out of the water. From that point, you are riding above the surface on the foil alone.

## What Does Riding an eFoil Feel Like?

First-time riders often describe it as a mix of snowboarding, surfing, and flying. Because the board is above the water, you do not feel chop or waves—the ride is remarkably smooth even in rough conditions.

The silence is the other surprise. With no combustion engine, the only sound is water flowing past the foil. Many riders say it feels meditative once they find their balance.

Most beginners start on their knees and progress to standing within 15–20 minutes of instruction. The wireless controller makes speed progression gradual and controlled.

## Who Can Ride an eFoil?

Almost anyone. You do not need surfing experience, athletic ability, or special training. If you can stand on a paddleboard, you can learn to eFoil.

That said, a professional instruction session is strongly recommended for first-timers. A good instructor covers board handling, water safety, speed control, and body position before you enter the water. With proper guidance, the learning curve is surprisingly gentle.

Children as young as 12 can ride with appropriate supervision and a beginner-friendly setup.

## Where Can You Ride an eFoil?

eFoils work in any body of water with enough depth for the hydrofoil wing—typically 1 metre or more. Popular locations include:

- **Oceans and seas** — Calm bays and lagoons are ideal. The Maldives, with its flat lagoons and warm turquoise water, is one of the best eFoil destinations in the world.
- **Lakes** — Flat water makes lakes excellent for learning.
- **Rivers** — Wider, calmer sections of rivers work well.

## Is eFoiling Safe?

Yes, when proper safety protocols are followed. Modern eFoils include multiple safety features:

- **Wireless kill switch** — Releasing the controller immediately cuts the motor.
- **Enclosed propeller** — The motor and propeller are shielded inside the mast, reducing injury risk.
- **Progressive speed control** — You control acceleration gradually, avoiding sudden speed changes.

All riders should wear a life vest and impact helmet. A safety briefing covering board handling, fall technique, and right-of-way rules is essential before every session. Read our full [eFoil safety guide](/blog/efoil-safety-guide) for more detail.
