## A New Chapter for Maldivian Watersports

The Maldives has long been synonymous with turquoise lagoons, world-class diving, and pristine beaches. But in recent years, a new scene has been quietly building across the atolls — one powered by wind, wings, and hydrofoils.

**Wingfoiling** — the sport of riding a hydrofoil board while holding an inflatable wing to harness the wind — has exploded globally since 2020. And the Maldives, with its unique combination of flat lagoons, warm water, and consistent trade winds, has emerged as one of the most compelling destinations on the planet to ride.

## Why the Maldives Is Perfect for Wingfoiling

### Flat Water, Everywhere

Unlike ocean-exposed coastlines where chop and swell make learning difficult, the Maldives offers thousands of square kilometers of sheltered lagoons. These flat-water playgrounds are ideal for beginners learning to foil and for advanced riders pushing freestyle and speed limits.

### Two Reliable Wind Seasons

The **southwest monsoon (Hulhangu)**, May to October, is the prime time for wing sports. Consistent 15–20+ knot winds power downwind runs between islands, lagoon cruising, and freestyle sessions. June is statistically the most reliable wind month, averaging 18 knots.

The **northeast monsoon (Iruvai)**, December to March, delivers lighter 8–13 knot conditions — enough for bigger wings (5m–6m) and relaxed freeriding. The water stays a constant 28–30°C year-round, so wetsuits are never needed.

### No Crowds, No Limits

With 1,190 islands spread across 90,000 km² of Indian Ocean, finding an uncrowded spot is never a problem. Many of the best lagoons are near uninhabited islands, where you can set up on a private sandbank and ride with nothing but reef sharks and sea turtles for company.

## Where to Ride

### Lhaviyani Atoll — The Wind Capital

Lhaviyani is fast becoming the go-to destination for kite and wing riders in the Maldives. Known for vast, shallow lagoons and consistent cross-shore winds, it offers ideal conditions for progression and freestyle. Kuredu and Cocoon are the key spots, and the annual **Kuredu Kite Week** (June) draws riders from around the region.

### Raa Atoll — The Emerging Gem

An emerging destination with new luxury resorts and pristine lagoons perfect for foiling. Raa Atoll's calm waters during the dry season also make it excellent for eFoil sessions.

### Thulusdhoo & Himmafushi — Surf-First, Wind-Growing

These North Male' islands are the heart of Maldivian surf culture — home to legendary breaks like Cokes and Jailbreaks. While they're surf-first destinations, the monsoon winds create excellent wing conditions in the channels and lagoons between islands.

### South Ari Atoll — Calm Water Paradise

Deep lagoons and calm waters make South Ari better for eFoiling and diving (including whale shark encounters) than wind sports, but on lighter wind days, the flat water is perfect for big-wing sessions.

## The Growing Local Scene

What makes the Maldives wingfoil story especially compelling is the local community. Maldivian riders — many of whom grew up surfing and kitesurfing — have embraced wingfoiling with serious passion.

The **Raalhu Edhuru Kite Fest** in Maafushi/Guraidhoo (September) is a community-driven celebration of wind sports and local talent. Local competitions are growing in frequency, and several Maldivian riders have begun competing internationally.

Schools and rental operators are expanding across the atolls. Where once you needed to bring your own gear, today there are multiple operators offering wing and kite equipment in the Malé atolls, Lhaviyani, and beyond.

## What's Next

The Maldivian government and tourism board are actively supporting watersports as a pillar of adventure tourism. Events like the **Four Seasons Surfing Champions Trophy**, the **Asian Surfing Championships**, and the **Visit Maldives Pro** (IBC World Tour bodyboarding) are putting the country on the global competitive map. Expect more wingfoil-specific events as the scene continues to grow.

If you're a wingfoiler looking for your next trip, or simply curious about what all the hype is about — the Maldives should be at the top of your list.
