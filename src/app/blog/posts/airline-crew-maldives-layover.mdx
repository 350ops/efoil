## You've Got 24 Hours in Maldives. Make Them Count.

If you're airline crew flying into Malé (MLE), you already know the drill: land, transfer to the hotel, sleep, maybe wander around Hulhumalé, and fly out again. Most layovers feel like a waiting room between flights.

But here's the thing — you're in the **Maldives**. One of the most beautiful places on Earth. And you've got a full free day.

This guide shows you exactly how to turn that layover into the best day of your trip.

---

## The 5-Hour Day Trip

There's a curated boat trip designed specifically for crew on layover. One trip, five hours, everything included:

### 1. Dolphin Swimming

The trip starts by cruising to the dolphin channel, where pods of wild spinner dolphins are found on 95% of trips. You'll swim alongside them in open water — no aquarium, no tanks, just you and wild dolphins in the Indian Ocean.

### 2. Reef Snorkeling

Two stops at pristine coral reefs teeming with life — tropical fish, sea turtles, reef sharks, and sometimes manta rays. All snorkeling gear is provided. The visibility in Maldives is extraordinary — you'll see 30+ meters in every direction.

### 3. Private Sandbank

A white sand strip in the middle of the ocean, just for your group. This is where you'll have a picnic lunch with drinks, swim in impossibly blue water, and take the kind of photos that make everyone back home reconsider their career choices.

### 4. Sunset Cruise

The return journey is timed for golden hour. Music, drinks, and the most spectacular sunset you've ever seen — from the middle of the Indian Ocean.
