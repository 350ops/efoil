## Why Safety Matters in eFoiling

An eFoil is a powerful piece of equipment. The board can reach speeds of 45+ km/h, and the hydrofoil wing beneath the surface is a rigid structure that can cause injury if handled incorrectly. With proper precautions, eFoiling is remarkably safe — but respecting the equipment and the water is non-negotiable.

This guide covers everything you need to know to ride safely, whether you are a first-timer or an experienced rider.

## Essential Safety Equipment

Every eFoil session should include:

### Life Vest (Impact Vest)
A Coast Guard-approved life vest or buoyancy aid is mandatory. It provides flotation if you fall and impact protection for your torso. Make sure it fits snugly without restricting arm movement.

### Helmet
An impact-rated water sport helmet protects your head in case of a fall near the board. This is especially important for beginners who are still learning to control speed and balance.

### Wetsuit or Rash Guard
Even in warm water like the Maldives (27–30°C), a rash guard protects against abrasion from the board surface and UV exposure during extended sessions.

### Whistle or Signal Device
A whistle attached to your life vest is a simple but effective signalling tool if you need assistance in the water.

## Pre-Ride Safety Checks

Before every session, run through this checklist:

1. **Battery level** — Ensure the battery is adequately charged. A low battery can cause unexpected power loss mid-ride.
2. **Wing and mast connections** — Check that all bolts and connections are tight. A loose hydrofoil can detach during riding.
3. **Propeller guard** — Confirm the propeller area is free of debris and the guard (if present) is secure.
4. **Controller pairing** — Test the wireless hand controller before entering the water. Confirm the kill-switch function works — releasing the trigger must immediately stop the motor.
5. **Leash** — Attach the board leash to your ankle or calf. This prevents the board from drifting away after a fall.
6. **Water conditions** — Check for currents, wind speed, obstacles (boats, swimmers, rocks), and water depth. The hydrofoil needs at least 1 metre of clearance.

## Riding Safety Rules

### Start Slow
Begin on your knees at the lowest speed setting. Get comfortable with the throttle response before attempting to stand. Gradual progression prevents crashes.

### Maintain a Safe Distance
Keep at least 30 metres from swimmers, other watercraft, docks, and shorelines. The hydrofoil extends below the surface and is not visible to others in the water.

### Ride in Appropriate Conditions
- **Wind:** Below 20 km/h is ideal. Strong winds affect balance and can push you into unsafe areas.
- **Visibility:** Avoid riding in fog, heavy rain, or low-light conditions.
- **Currents:** Be aware of tidal currents, especially near channels and reef passes.

### Know How to Fall
Falls are inevitable, especially while learning. The safest technique:
- **Fall flat, not feet-first.** A flat fall distributes impact across your body. Falling feet-first near the board risks contact with the hydrofoil.
- **Fall away from the board.** Push yourself sideways, away from the mast and wing.
- **Release the controller immediately.** This cuts the motor and prevents the board from accelerating after you fall.

### Never Ride Under the Influence
Alcohol and eFoiling do not mix. Impaired reaction time and balance significantly increase injury risk.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Too much throttle too soon** — The most common mistake. Sudden acceleration throws you off balance. Use gentle, gradual throttle input.
2. **Stiff legs** — Locked knees absorb no impact and make corrections difficult. Keep your knees slightly bent and weight centred.
3. **Looking down** — Focus on the horizon, not the board. Looking down shifts your centre of gravity forward and causes nose-dives.
4. **Ignoring wind direction** — Always start riding into the wind. Riding downwind accelerates you faster than you expect.
5. **Skipping the briefing** — Every rider, regardless of experience, should receive a location-specific safety briefing covering local hazards, currents, and emergency procedures.

## Emergency Procedures

### If You Lose the Controller
The eFoil will stop immediately when the controller signal is lost. Swim to the board (it will be attached via leash) and retrieve the controller from the water.

### If the Battery Dies Mid-Ride
The board remains buoyant without power. Lie on the board and paddle back to your starting point, or signal for assistance.

### If You Are Caught in a Current
Do not fight the current. Lie flat on the board to reduce drag and paddle at an angle toward shore or your boat. Use your whistle to signal for help if needed.

### If Someone Is Injured
- Stop all eFoil activity immediately.
- Provide flotation support to the injured person.
- Signal for help (whistle, wave arms, call over radio).
- Do not attempt to move someone with a suspected spinal injury.

## Why Professional Instruction Matters

A 15-minute instruction session prevents the vast majority of eFoil incidents. A qualified instructor covers:
- Board and controller handling
- Body positioning and weight transfer
- Speed progression and throttle control
- Location-specific hazards
- Emergency procedures
